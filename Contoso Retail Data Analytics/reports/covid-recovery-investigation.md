# COVID-19 Impact & Recovery Investigation Report

- **Analysis Date:** 2026-06-29 21:47
- **Data Period:** 2016-01-01 to 2021-02-20
- **COVID Split Date:** March 1, 2020

---

## 1. Executive Summary

Contoso Retail experienced a severe COVID-19 impact starting March 2020. Total revenue dropped **87.9%** from $49,717,890 (4+ years pre-COVID) to $6,037,590 (post-COVID). Key findings:

- **Revenue decline:** -87.9% overall
- **Customer churn:** 81.7% of pre-COVID customers did not return
- **Spend drop:** Even retained customers cut spending by ~48%
- **Margin resilience:** Profit margins held steady at ~58% across all categories
- **Recovery was weak:** H2 2020 revenue reached only 17-43% of H1 2019 levels per category

### 2.2 Growth Drivers (Pre-COVID, 2016-2019)

**Computers** drove **48.3%** of all revenue growth from 2016 to 2019, growing from $1.5M to $7.0M (+265%). Cell Phones added 16.6% and Cameras added 11.6% of total growth.

### 2.3 COVID Crash Depth (Q1 2020 pre-crash vs Q2 2020)

All categories dropped 50-68% immediately after COVID hit:
- **Hardest hit:** Audio (-68.1%)
- **Least hit:** Cameras and camcorders (-50.8%)

### 2.4 Recovery by H2 2020 (vs H1 2019 baseline)

- **Best recovery:** Music, Movies & Audio Books (42.7% of pre-COVID)
- **Worst recovery:** Home Appliances (17.6%)
- **Computers** recovered to 33.7% of pre-COVID level

### 2.5 Profit Margin Resilience

Remarkably, profit margins remained stable across ALL categories (within +/- 1 percentage point pre vs post COVID). This suggests the business did not resort to discounting during the crisis.

## 4. Customer Behavior Changes

### 4.1 Customer Base Shrinking

- **9,248** of 11,323 pre-COVID customers churned (**81.7%**)
- Only **2,075** customers (18.3%) continued buying after COVID
- Acquired only **564** new customers post-COVID

### 4.2 Spending Decline

- Pre-COVID avg spend per customer: **$4,390.88**
- Post-COVID avg spend per customer: **$2,287.83**
- Even **retained customers** cut spending by ~48% (from $4,889.59 to $2,339.51)

### 4.3 Customer Quality Shift

- Avg spend of **churned** customers: $4,278.98
- Avg spend of **retained** customers: $7,229.10
- Avg spend of **new** customers: $2,097.70

## 5. Key Insights & Recommendations

### Key Insights

1. **Computers category is the growth engine** -- drove 48% of pre-COVID growth and should be the focus of recovery efforts
2. **Customer retention is the #1 problem** -- 82% churn rate means the customer base almost completely turned over
3. **Margin stability is a bright spot** -- no fire-sale discounting occurred, preserving profitability
4. **Online channel slightly more resilient** (-84.9% vs -88.6% physical) but still severely impacted
5. **New customers are lower value** ($2,098 avg) compared to churned customers ($4,279) -- acquisition quality needs attention
6. **Italy was the hardest hit market** (-94.2%) -- may need market-specific recovery strategy

### Recommendations

1. **Win-back retained customers:** 2,075 retained customers spend $7,229 avg -- these are the most valuable; run loyalty programs
2. **Revive the Computers category** -- it was the growth driver before COVID; marketing campaigns should lead with Computers
3. **Re-activate Audio & Home Appliances** -- these had the worst recovery rates (17-20%) and may need promotions
4. **Improve customer acquisition quality** -- new customers spend 51% less than churned ones; target higher-LTV segments
5. **Italy market review** -- the -94.2% drop may indicate structural issues beyond COVID (e.g., supply chain, competition)

---

## 6. Data Caveats

- Post-COVID period (Mar 2020 - Feb 2021) is shorter than pre-COVID (Jan 2016 - Feb 2020)
- 2021 data is partial (only Jan-Feb), so post-COVID comparisons should be treated as directional
- Pre/Post split at March 1 is a simplification; lockdowns varied by country timeline
- Customer churn calculation does not account for customers who may return later
