## 3. Pre-COVID Growth Story (2016-2019)

Before the pandemic, Contoso experienced steady growth:

- Revenue grew **+163%** from $6.9M (2016) to $18.3M (2019)
- Best month was **December 2019** ($2,477,295.85)
- **Computers** category drove **48%** of all revenue growth -- from $1.5M to $7.0M (+265%)
- **Cell Phones** contributed 16.6% of growth

## 4. COVID-19 Impact Analysis

### 4.1 Revenue Crash

- **Pre-COVID monthly avg:** $946,282.48

### 4.3 Customer Impact

- **Customer churn:** 81.7% of pre-COVID customers (9,248 out of 11,323) did not return
- **Retained:** Only 2,075 customers (18.3%) continued buying
- **Spend decline per retained customer:** -48% (from $4,890 to $2,340)
- **New customer quality:** New customers spend $2,098 avg vs churned $4,279 -- 51% lower

### 4.4 Country Impact

- **Italy** was hardest hit (-94.2%) -- likely due to early/severe lockdown
- **France** was least impacted (-84.3%)
- **Online** (-84.9%) was more resilient than **Physical** (-88.6%)
- All markets crashed in Q2 2020 and none recovered to pre-COVID levels

### 4.5 Recovery Forecast

- **Post-COVID trend (Mar-Dec 2020):** Slightly declining ($13,764/month, R2=0.055)
- **H2 2020 trend:** Very slight recovery (+$2,257/month)
- **Early 2021 signal:** Jan-Feb avg $519,644 is 35% above Q4 2020, but still only 55% of pre-COVID baseline
- **At current H2 2020 recovery rate, full recovery would take ~240 months** (20 years)
- **Conclusion:** The business has not shown meaningful recovery within the data period

## 10. Data Caveats & Limitations

- **2021 data is partial** (only Jan-Feb) -- all growth comparisons note which years are complete vs partial
- **79.1% missing delivery dates** -- delivery analysis based on only 20.9% of orders, may be biased
- **Customer churn** pre/post COVID comparison spans different time lengths (4+ years vs ~1 year)
- **Revenue calculation** uses Unit Price USD (list price) -- actual transaction prices may vary with discounts/promotions
- **RFM segmentation** was not split by pre/post COVID period, which could mask changes in segment behavior
- **Forecast** is based on simple linear projection (R-squared 0.055) -- actual recovery may follow different patterns
